01 · About

Background &
Expertise

I am an Assistant Professor of Computer Science at Xavier University in Cincinnati, Ohio. My work sits at the crossroads of cybersecurity, data privacy, and empirical social science — fields I believe are inseparable in an era of pervasive digital surveillance and algorithmic decision-making.

My doctoral research developed a three-study empirical project examining social media privacy behavior through the lens of Protection Motivation Theory (PMT). This work spanned taxonomy development, multi-group structural equation modeling across major platforms, and a longitudinal video intervention experiment.

Beyond research, I am passionate about building students' capacity for ethical, critical thinking in computing. My courses integrate hands-on labs, capture-the-flag challenges, and socially grounded frameworks that connect technical skills to real-world consequences.

02 · Research

Selected
Work

01

Privacy Taxonomy & Boundary Objects

Adapting Solove's Privacy Taxonomy as a boundary object for multi-disciplinary privacy research; multi-label methodology for categorizing social media privacy violations.

02

Multi-Group SEM of Privacy Feature Usage

Nationally representative (N = 574) multi-group structural equation modeling of privacy feature adoption across Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, and LinkedIn, replicating and extending Page et al.

03

Longitudinal PMT Video Intervention ACM CHI

A longitudinal experiment testing Protection Motivation Theory–grounded video interventions on social media privacy protective behavior, published at ACM CHI.

03 · Teaching

Courses

CSCI 170

Computer Science I

Foundational programming concepts in Python. Topics include problem solving and algorithms, machine architecture, operating systems, assembly language, higher level programming languages, compilers, limits of computation, networking, applications, and social/ethical issues.

CSCI 180

Computer Science II

Foundational programming concepts in Java covering object-oriented design, advanced syntax of a programming language; dynamic memory; recursion; sorting; searching; stacks; queues; social and ethical issues related to software design and reliability.

CSCI 200

Socially Responsible Data Use

An ethics-forward course exploring data privacy, algorithmic bias, and societal impact of computing decisions. Includes a Data Ethics Impact Report project with mastery grading.
